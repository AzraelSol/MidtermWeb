Strawberry
Capital

Baguio is famous for its strawberries, which thrive in the cool climate of the region. Visitors can enjoy picking fresh strawberries at strawberry farms or savoring strawberry-based delicacies such as jams, tarts, and ice cream.

Burnham
Park

a 32.84-hectare urban park in Baguio City, named after American architect Daniel Burnham. It offers boating on a man-made lake, gardens, playgrounds, and cultural events, symbolizing the city's heritage and urban planning.

Pine
Trees

Baguio City is known for its picturesque landscape adorned with pine trees. The city's moniker "City of Pines" reflects this distinctive feature, with pine trees lining its roads and parks, creating a refreshing and serene ambiance.

Stilted
Structures

A common architectural element in mountain homes is the wooden platforms or stilts that raise the homes above the earth. Elevating the homes facilitates ventilation and airflow to control interior temperature and humidity while also providing protection against flooding, landslides, and pests.

Mines View
Park

One of the most iconic landmarks in Baguio City is Mines View Park, which offers panoramic views of abandoned gold and copper mines in the nearby mountains. Visitors can also enjoy horseback riding, souvenir shopping, and traditional Igorot photo opportunities wearing traditional attire.
